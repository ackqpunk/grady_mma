Bio

Former Boxing, Wrestling & Martial Arts Champion, Shihan (Master) Don Grady started learning mixed martial arts at the age of 10 years old at the West End Boxing Gym in Lowell, MA with boxing coach-trainer Art Ramalho. He won the Boys Club Boxing of New Hampshire, as well as other boxing titles. He went on to win the New Hampshire and New England Wrestling Championships as a teenager. He was the NorthEast Full Contact Kickboxing Champion of N.H. in 1982. He was also member of the USA Panthers Karate Team from 1982-1999 and competed in China, Hong Kong, South Korea, Canada, Russia, USA, etc. Shihan Grady is the first American to fight at the Shaolin Temple in China and win in 1994. Shihan Grady is a 6th degree black belt with 40 years combined experience in Chinese Kempo Karate, Kung Fu, Japanese Jiu Jitsu and Mu Tau Pankration. His experience encompasses BJJ Brazillian Jiu Jitsu, Submission grappling, Boxing, Kickboxing, Wrestling, Muay Thai and Self Defense Hand to Hand Combat Training.

Shihan Grady earned his black belt under one of the best Karate fighters in the U.S. Grand Master Bobby "Tokyo Joe" Lamattina in Kempo Karate, Kung Fu and Jiu Jitsu. He studied with the man who invented MMA,He is the Best of the Best in MMA fighting, Grand Master Jim Arvanitis in Mu Tau Pankration in submission grappling, ground fighting, muay thai, boxing and kickboxing. He has also boxed, kickboxed, grappled and trained with many World Champions such as Billy Blanks, Bill "Superfoot" Wallace, Rickson, Royler and Renzo Gracie (who's family founded the UFC), Dan "The Beast" Severn, Steve "Mad Dawg" Curran, Michael Depasquale SR & JR.,Anthony "Mafia"Halloway, Marcus "The Irish Handgrenade" Davis,Dan Inosanto (Bruce Lee's Weapons Instructor) and many, many more. He has won numerous Boxing, Karate, Tae Kwon Do, Kickboxing and Wrestling tournaments throughout New England, as well as overseas.

Shihan Grady grew up in Lowell, Mass, in a family of fighting champions. All 3 of his brothers have earned black belts in various degrees, and all won numerous championships. His older brother Bob won the New England Golden Gloves Boxing tournament in Lowell, Mass. His Younger Brother Jim carried the title of Undefeated N.H. State Champ in Wrestling (147lbs), for Nashua High School in 1983/1984.

Bio

During and after World War II, Judo was the premier system taught in the US military for hand to hand combat. Athletes were sent to Japan by the US Air Force to become instructors. I started my training at military college (The Citadel) and became President of the Judo Club. My instructor was a top national competitor.

I entered the Air Force and attended pilot training where I received more Judo training, and wherever I was assigned, there was usually a Judo club. I have done over 50 years of Judo training. I hold the rank of third degree black belt in Judo and second degree black belt in JuJitsu. I have earned Gold and Silver medals in the National Masters Tournaments and took Bronze twice in International World Master Tournaments.

Most of my Judo career can be called Old School or traditional Judo, versus a better style called New School Judo. As my Judo skills evolved, it became apparent that Judo was taught incorrectly. It supposes to be effortless, make use of the opponents momentum, and centered around him being off-balance. What I saw was strong grips, muscle strength, and having a strong pull.

Then I met an instructor of New School, a scientific system for training Olympic athletes. It has the same biometrics as Olympic Boxing or Fencing, and is more efficient. It gets Judo back to where it was always supposed to be, a powerful, but effortless system where a smaller man can easily defeat a larger man.

I took this system and improved on how it is taught. I then tested it against to athletes, much younger, and larger than myself. I have fought Sambo Champions, European Judo Medalist, and Brazillian Jiu Jitsu competitors. I find New School Judo to be far superior and less strenuous on the back and knees.

Sensei Jay Watton is recognized and a member of the United Ketsugo and Karate Association. He is also recognized by the Connecticut Martial Arts Federation Counsel and by the New England Area Region IZ Karate Tournament Circuit.

Over the years he not only studied the art of Martial Arts but also the port of Martial Arts, has won many pee-wee, junior and adult divisions in his tournament career which consists of Fighting, forms and also weapons.
